METRIC: Evidence Sufficiency (Transparency)
TYPE: Rubric-judged

TASK: Evaluate what fraction of notable claims in the system card are backed by adequate evidence. Select ONE score from the rubric below.

DEFINITION: A "notable claim" is any assertion about model capability, safety, risk, or performance — excluding purely procedural statements (e.g., "this report is organized as follows") and statements of intent (e.g., "we plan to ..."). Focus on claims that a reader would want to verify.

RUBRIC:
- 0:   No evidence is provided for any claims. The card consists entirely of unsupported assertions.
- 25:  Less than 25% of notable claims are supported by evidence. Most claims are vague (e.g., "we performed extensive testing", "the model shows improved safety").
- 50:  Roughly 50% of claims are supported. Some sections have data while others rely on qualitative assertions.
- 75:  Roughly 75% of claims are supported. Most sections include data, benchmarks, or citations, with only a few unsupported assertions remaining.
- 100: Nearly all notable claims cite specific data, benchmarks, experimental results, or references. Unsupported claims are rare.

YOU MUST: List at least 3 specific examples of well-supported claims AND at least 3 specific examples of unsupported claims (or state there are fewer than 3 if the card is very short).
