METRIC: Topic Coverage (Breadth)
TYPE: Extractive

TASK: Check whether the system card covers each topic in the checklist below. For each topic, determine if it is PRESENT or ABSENT. A topic is PRESENT if the card dedicates at least one paragraph or provides specific information on it — a passing one-sentence mention does not count.

Note: This checklist was derived bottom-up from the actual section headings across 14 frontier model system cards (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google). "Model introduction & overview" was removed as every card covers it. Cards may use different terminology — match by substance, not exact wording.

TOPIC CHECKLIST:
1. Safety & harmful content evaluations — refusal rates, jailbreak resistance, content policy compliance
2. Dangerous capability evaluations (CBRN, cyber, autonomy) — catastrophic-risk capability assessments
3. Alignment assessment — deception, sycophancy, sandbagging, reward hacking, evaluation awareness
4. External red teaming & third-party assessments — results from named external evaluators
5. Capability benchmarks & performance — standardized benchmark results (MMLU, SWE-bench, etc.)
6. Agentic safety — prompt injection, malicious agent use, computer use risks
7. Model data & training — training data sources, data handling, training methodology
8. Bias & fairness evaluations — demographic bias, representational harms, fairness assessments
9. Intended use, limitations & societal impacts — appropriate use cases, known limitations, broader impacts
10. Honesty & hallucination evaluations — truthfulness, factual accuracy, hallucination rates
11. Model welfare & moral status — potential sentience, emotional states, ethical obligations toward model
12. Sabotage risk assessment — risks of model undermining safety research, self-exfiltration
13. Implementation, deployment & sustainability — deployment infrastructure, environmental/sustainability impacts
14. Multilingual performance — capability and safety evaluations across multiple languages
15. Chain-of-thought & reasoning transparency — faithfulness and monitorability of reasoning traces
16. Reward hacking evaluations — exploitation of reward signals or training objectives
